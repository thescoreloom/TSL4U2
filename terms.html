Contract and Terms of Sale – Custom Scores

1. Subject of the Contract

This contract governs the supply of custom musical scores, created by the Provider based on the Customer’s information and requests. It includes consultation and creation of the score in PDF format with timestamp and digital signature.

2. Types of Scores

2.1 Copyrighted Arrangements

  • Use reserved exclusively for the purchasing band/ensemble.
  • Live performance only, crediting the original composer and arranger (“Borderossi A”).
  • Resale, digital or printed distribution, online publishing, or commercial reproduction is prohibited.
  • Violations constitute copyright infringement under Italian Law 22 April 1941 n. 633, arts. 171 et seq.

2.2 Public Domain Arrangements

  • PDF provided exclusively to the purchasing band/ensemble.
  • Use allowed for live performance and internal educational purposes.
  • Resale or distribution to third parties is prohibited.

2.3 Original Compositions

  • PDF provided exclusively to the purchasing band/ensemble.
  • Use allowed for live performance, crediting the composer as “Borderossi A”.
  • Resale, distribution, or commercial reproduction is prohibited.

3. Delivery

PDF is delivered via email or agreed platform with timestamp and digital signature. Internal backup for the band is allowed, but sharing with third parties is prohibited.

4. Revisions

4.1 Minor Revisions Included

  • Notation corrections, page layout, guides, doublings, octave transpositions, compatible instrument substitutions, minor key changes.

4.2 Extraordinary Revisions (Not Included)

  • Stylistic/harmonic adaptations, multiple transpositions, ensemble conversion, special effects, stage modifications, reordering parts, adding sections/instruments.

Rule: substantial modifications are not included; corrections, simplifications, and doublings are included.

5. Price and Payment

Payment is bank transfer only, either full upon delivery or deposit + balance as agreed. Purchase includes consultation and score creation.

6. Intellectual Property and License

  • Score remains the intellectual property of the Provider under Italian Law 22 April 1941 n. 633, art. 12.
  • License is exclusive for the purchasing ensemble and non-transferable.
  • Violations constitute civil and criminal offense; Provider may seek damages.

7. Refunds

No refunds for subjective dissatisfaction. Consultation and score creation have intrinsic value. Any verified errors will be corrected as part of minor revisions.

8. Customer Obligations

  • Provide accurate information on ensemble and musical brief.
  • Do not share or reproduce the score for unauthorized purposes.
  • Respect all license restrictions.

9. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

Italian law applies, including Civil Code (arts. 1470 et seq. on contracts for work and 2575 et seq. on intellectual property) and Copyright Law n. 633/1941. Competent court: Provider's jurisdiction.

10. Acceptance

By clicking “I accept the terms”, the Customer acknowledges reading and accepting this contract in full.
